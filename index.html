Welcome to the Best Place on Earth for Pokemon Enthusiasts.

What?

These are initial visualizations and results on the problem of classifying Pokemon by certain attributes.

How??

I've combed through the Pokedex extensively to record these findings about Pokemon classification.

Then, I dumped all of my data into a Jupyter Notebook and went to town.

Why?!?

Let's check just how uniform Nintendo and GameFreak were in distributing the different features to different Pokemon!

Pokemon Stats: Can we predict them?

There are 6 Pokemon Stats, HP (health points), Attack, Defense, Special Attack, Special Defense, and Speed. On average, a given stat hovers around 70 pts, with roughly 75% of Pokemon with average stats below 100 pts and above 50 pts.

We certainly have some outliers, with each stat having a minimum of around 10 and a maximumn of around 200. Of course, this is easily explained by the "baby"/initial form Pokemon and, on the other side of the spectrum, the legendary Pokemon.

Babies and legends aside, the summary statistics remain essentially the same, just with a slight decrease in variuance. More on PokeStats if you hit the link below!

What else did we dig up?

Other than a look at how the stats interact with themselves, I looked at a few possible indicators. We compared the stats to:

  • Pokemon Weight and Height
  • Pokemon Type
  • Pokemon Generation

I wrapped up my analysis with a quick look into the number of Legendary or Mythical Pokemon and the number of multi-form Pokemon introduced per Generation, just to peek into the distribution of such Pokemon.
