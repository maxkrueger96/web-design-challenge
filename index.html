Gotta Catch 'Em All!

Welcome to the Best Place on Earth for Pokemon Enthusiasts.

What?

These are initial visualizations and results on the problem of classifying Pokemon using certain attributes. Given a Pokemon's size, color, type, etc, can I accurately predict:

  • How high its stats are?
  • How well does its stats correlate with its other characteristics?
  • Are stats distributed evenly among all of the several different varieties of Pokemon?

How??

I've combed through the Pokedex extensively to record these findings about Pokemon classification.

Then, I dumped all of my data into a Jupyter Notebook and went to town.

Why?!?

Let's check just how uniform Nintendo and GameFreak were in distributing the different features to different Pokemon!

Pokemon Stats:

Can we predict them?


There are 6 Pokemon Stats, HP (health points), Attack, Defense, Special Attack, Special Defense, and Speed. On average, a given stat hovers around 70 pts, with roughly 75% of Pokemon with average stats below 100 pts and above 50 pts.

We certainly have some outliers, with each stat having a minimum of around 10 and a maximumn of around 200. Of course, this is easily explained by the "baby"/initial form Pokemon and, on the other side of the spectrum, the legendary Pokemon.

Babies and legends aside, the summary statistics remain essentially the same, just with a slight decrease in variance. After a bit of data cleaning, I started to investiagte how the six stats interact with one another. For example, does a Pokemon's attack stat tell me anything about that Pokemon's speed?

What did we dig up?


Other than a look at how the stats interact with themselves, I looked at a few possible indicators. We compared the stats to:

  • Pokemon Weight
  • Pokemon Height
  • Pokemon Type
  • Pokemon Generation

Overall, my analysis points to this:

It is unlikely, given a Pokemon's weight, height, type, or other stat,that you can accurately predict one of the stats of an inlier Pokemon. The combinations of trait and stat are, in general, too uniform.
Most plots created for this project have no significant correlations, and it would not make a lot of sense to separate any variables that behave uniquely. Ultimately, this was my hypothesis going in; I imagine it is a goal of the developers to have a somewhat uniform spread of weak, average, and strong pokemon, light and heavy pokemon, short and tall pokemon, etc.

I also took a slightly off-route turn by looking at the number of Legendary or Mythical Pokemon and the number of multi-form Pokemon introduced per Generation, just to peek into the distribution of such Pokemon, and see if the trends matched up in any generation I also looked at the color diversity among the types.
